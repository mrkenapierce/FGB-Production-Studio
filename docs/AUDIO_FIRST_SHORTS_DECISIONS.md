# Audio-First Shorts — Implementation Decisions

Date: July 18, 2026

## Approved defaults

- Shared workflow for Football's Greatest Bears, Football's Greatest Bars, EPIC Communities, and future channels.
- WAV is the standard audio source.
- Default batch is three premium Shorts plus five rapid-output Shorts.
- Tactical treatment is selected automatically only when the narration contains sufficient scheme, formation, coverage, route, matchup, protection, or similar strategic language.
- Visual inputs may include AI-generated images, licensed stock visuals, public-domain material, and user-supplied photographs.
- The locked one-word caption protocol remains: `#C83803`, black outline, black shadow, condensed bold italic typography, and lower-middle safe placement.

## Production model

1. Transcribe the WAV automatically when no timed transcript is supplied.
2. Rank coherent 20–58 second windows and prevent substantial overlap or duplicate ideas.
3. Assign the first three candidates to premium production slots.
4. Convert the strongest qualifying tactical candidate into an explainer slot; keep other premium slots in the animated editorial/newspaper treatment.
5. Assign the remaining five candidates to rapid quote-driven graphics.
6. Render each Short as a separate 1080 × 1920 MP4 with separate metadata and caption files.

## Visual behavior

- The source video's appearance is not reconstructed or imitated.
- Optional visual assets are matched by filename keywords when possible.
- The workflow remains fully functional without supplied imagery by creating branded procedural editorial scenes.
- No NFL or team logos are generated by the workflow.
- Channel profiles control watermark, headline language, hashtags, and closing treatment.

## Security and transcription

- A supplied SRT, VTT, or timestamped TXT transcript bypasses external transcription.
- WAV-only runs use the OpenAI transcription endpoint with word and segment timestamps.
- Long WAV files are converted to mono transcription sections before upload and timestamps are reassembled with offsets.
- The desktop application stores the API key only through Electron secure storage when available, or reads `OPENAI_API_KEY` from the environment.

## Future expansion

- Candidate review and editable in/out points before rendering.
- Built-in image-generation requests for approved premium clips.
- Stock-library search and licensing records.
- Face-aware or object-aware motion treatment for supplied images.
- Channel-specific visual packs beyond the three current profiles.
